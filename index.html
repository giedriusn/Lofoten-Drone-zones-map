Drone rules — under 250 g

Norway, open category A1. These operational rules apply on top of the zones on the map.

Verified against primary law — EU 2019/947 & 2019/945, the Norwegian forskrift om ubemannede luftfartøyer (BSL A 7-2, FOR-2024-11-01-2777), naturmangfoldloven, and Datatilsynet guidance. A reference, not legal advice — confirm before flying.

① Aircraft, registration & age

  • Register as operator at flydrone.no if your drone has a camera/sensor — required even under 250 g. Only camera-free or certified-toy drones are exempt. Put your operator number on the drone. EU 2019/947 Art 14(5),(8)
  • No exam or course needed for a sub-250 g (C0 / self-built) drone — just know the manual. EU 2019/947 UAS.OPEN.020(4)
  • Min. pilot age 16 — no minimum for a self-built <250 g drone, a toy-class C0, or when flying under supervision of someone 16+. EU 2019/947 Art 9
  • Insurance not legally required under 250 g (open category); ≥250 g needs ≥750 000 SDR third-party cover. Still recommended. BSL A 7-2 §6 · luftfartsloven §11-2 · FOR-2004-07-06-1101 §4

② In the air

  • Max 120 m above the closest point of the ground (the ceiling follows the terrain over hills/valleys). EU 2019/947 UAS.OPEN.010(2) · Art 4(1)(e)
  • Keep it in sight (VLOS) at all times — FPV goggles require a spotter. EU 2019/947 Art 4(1)(d) · UAS.OPEN.060(2)(b)
  • People: A1 lets you overfly individual uninvolved people, but never assemblies / crowds. EU 2019/947 UAS.OPEN.020(2)
  • C0 design limits: <250 g, ≤19 m/s. EU 2019/945 Annex Part 1

③ Where you can't fly (shown on the map)

  • No closer than 5 km to an airport without permission from the air traffic service. BSL A 7-2 §7
  • Not over/near military areas & vessels, embassies or prisons without the local commander's permission. BSL A 7-2 §7
  • Respect control & traffic zones (CTR/TIZ), restricted & danger areas — all the airspace layers.
  • Filming designated military/prohibited areas needs NSM permission. FOR-2018-06-22-951 §6 · lov 2017-06-21-88

④ Wildlife & nature (applies regardless of weight)

  • Don't disturb wildlife — anywhere. "Ved enhver aktivitet skal unødig skade og lidelse på viltlevende dyr og deres reir, bo eller hi unngås." naturmangfoldloven §15 (+ §6)
  • Banned in national parks and most large landscape areas, and in many nature reserves — where a verneforskrift bans "modellfly", that includes drones (take-off, landing and flying in). Miljødirektoratet · nml §77
  • Seabird reserves: seasonal access ban (ferdselsforbud), commonly 15 Apr–31 Jul during nesting — exact dates per area. e.g. Bliksvær FOR-2002-12-06-1426 §3

⑤ Privacy (camera)

  • GDPR / personopplysningsloven applies when you capture identifiable people or property. Purely private use is exempt, but you generally need consent to publish footage of identifiable people, and must never film people in their home/garden without permission. Be visible; inform people nearby. Datatilsynet · GDPR Art 2(2)(c)

Always confirm on flydrone.no / the SafeToFly app and check NOTAMs before flying.

What do the zones mean?

The colours on the map, in plain words — why you can't fly, and what to do about it.

OK ≤120 m Just fly — nothing to do. Fly carefully Allowed without permission — just be careful. Need permission You may fly, but only after someone says yes. Check NOTAM Only blocked when activated — check NOTAMs that day. No-fly Don't — easiest is to pick another spot.
  • Airport — 5 km zoneNeed permissionPlanes take off and land here — a drone could hit one.To fly: phone the airport's control tower and get their OK. No OK → don't fly.
  • Control zone (CTR)Need permissionA control tower manages all flying right around a larger airport, from the ground up.To fly: contact the tower and get clearance before take-off.
  • Traffic info zone (TIZ)Need permissionA large advisory zone around a smaller airfield — no tower, just an information service (AFIS). It looks huge on the map (often 20–30 km across), but it's softer than a control zone.To fly: call the airport's information service and coordinate before flying.
  • Restricted areaNo-flyThe authorities limit or ban flying here (e.g. sensitive sites).To fly: needs special permission from the body that set it — usually not worth it. Pick another spot.
  • Danger area (firing / military)No-flyLive shooting, rocket launches and similar — dangerous, but active only at set times.To fly: check live NOTAMs ↗; if it's active that day, fly somewhere else.
  • Military exercise areaCheck NOTAMThe big areas over the sea. Used during military exercises only — most days they're empty and fine.To fly: check live NOTAMs ↗ for the day. No exercise on → you're OK. Exercise on → stay out.
  • Air sports areaFly carefullyParagliders, skydivers or small planes use this air — you could crash into them.To fly: go ahead, no permission needed — just watch for them and give way.
  • Nature reserve / national parkNo-flyProtected nature. Drones scare animals — especially nesting birds — so flying is usually banned.To fly: you'd need an exemption from the county governor (Statsforvalteren) — rarely given. Pick another spot.
  • PrisonNeed permissionFlying over or near a prison needs permission — the law sets no fixed distance, so the ring is just a "keep clear" guide.To fly: get the facility's / local authority's OK first, or pick another spot.
  • Hospital / HEMS helipadFly carefullyAir-ambulance helicopters land here — low, fast and with no warning.To fly: allowed, no permission needed — stay clear and land at once if a helicopter comes.
  • Populated areaFly carefullyTowns and houses.To fly: allowed, no permission needed — just don't fly over crowds and keep your distance from people.
  • Controlled airspace (high)OK ≤120 mAirliner routes high in the sky — they sit above the 120 m drone limit.To fly: nothing to do — you're fine as long as you stay under 120 m.

Two numbers to remember

  • 120 mThe highest your drone may go — about 400 ft above the ground below it.
  • 5 kmHow far to stay from airports unless you have permission.

Why these exist: drones share the sky with planes, helicopters and wildlife. These zones keep everyone safe. When in doubt, don't fly — check flydrone.no.
